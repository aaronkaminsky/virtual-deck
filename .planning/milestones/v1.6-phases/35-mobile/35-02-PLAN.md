  <how-to-verify>
    Run through this checklist in order. Report ALL findings to the operator before resuming.

    **Pre-flight**

    1. Confirm dev servers are running. From two terminals:
       - Terminal A: `npm run dev` (PartyKit, port 1999)
       - Terminal B: `npm run dev:client` (Vite, port 5173)
       If either is missing, start it. The pre-push git hook also requires both to be live for e2e tests; this gives you the same setup.

    2. Run the full mobile spec to confirm Plan 01 is GREEN end-to-end:
       `npm run test:e2e -- playwright/mobile.spec.ts`
       All 5 tests must pass. If any fail, stop here and report — the issue belongs in Plan 01, not 02.

    **Visual verification at 375×667 (Chrome DevTools, iPhone SE preset or manual 375×667 device toolbar)**

    3. Open `http://localhost:5173/?room=test35` in Chrome. Enable DevTools, switch to device mode at exactly 375×667. Join as "MobileTest".

    4. With NO cards on canvas yet:
       - Confirm: hand zone is visible at the bottom of the viewport.
       - Confirm: spread zone (your personal play area, above the hand) is visible.
       - Confirm: no horizontal page scroll (`document.documentElement.scrollWidth === 375`).
       - Confirm: no vertical page scroll required to reach hand (page scrollHeight ≤ clientHeight).
       - Confirm: no edge arrows are visible anywhere (empty canvas = no overflow).

    5. Deal 5 cards (open controls hamburger, enter 5, click Deal). Verify hand and spread are still visible after dealing.

    6. **max-h tuning step**: Measure the canvas band height in DevTools. With the canvas at `max-h-[240px]`:
       - If spread + hand are still both fully visible below the canvas → 240 is correct. SKIP step 7.
       - If spread top is clipped, or hand is pushed off-screen, or there is significant unused space above spread → 240 needs adjustment. PROCEED to step 7.

    7. **If tuning is required:** measure the actual rendered heights of opponent area + spread + hand in DevTools. Determine the correct `max-h` value such that:
       - At 375×667, all bands fit within the viewport without page scroll
       - The canvas band is as tall as possible while preserving the above
       Acceptable range: 200–280px. If your measurement is outside this range, stop and report — something else is mis-sized.
       Edit `src/components/BoardView.tsx`: change `max-h-[240px]` to `max-h-[Npx]` where N is your measured value. Keep `sm:max-h-none`. Save. Confirm in DevTools that the new value resolves the clipping/spacing.

    **Edge arrow + pan verification (still at 375×667)**

    8. Drag a hand card to the canvas at far right (release near x=350). Confirm:
       - The right edge arrow appears: a frosted 32×80 rectangle on the right edge with a `‹` glyph rotated 180° (visible as `›`-shaped).
       - The down edge arrow may also appear if the card lands at the bottom of the capped canvas band.

    9. With your mouse, press-and-hold the right arrow for ~1 second. Confirm:
       - Canvas inner content slides LEFT continuously while held.
       - On release, panning stops immediately.
       - After scrolling right, the LEFT edge arrow now appears (because `scroll.x > 0`).

    10. Press-and-hold the left arrow. Confirm canvas pans back. Continue until scroll.x reaches 0. The left arrow disappears (overflow no longer present in that direction).

    **Drop-under-pan verification**

    11. Drag a hand card to the canvas. Without releasing, observe the card position is at the visual location of your cursor. Release — the card drops.

    12. Pan the canvas right by ~80px (hold right arrow ~150ms). Now drag another hand card to the canvas and release at any visible point. Confirm:
        - The card lands at the visual drop point.
        - Pan back to scroll.x = 0 (hold left arrow). The card remains where it appears now — its canvas-space coordinates are correct (NOT shifted by `-scrollX` from where you dropped). If the card jumps to a different spot when panning back, the scroll offset math in BoardDragLayer is wrong.

    **Touch-only verification (real device or DevTools touch emulation)**

    13. Enable "Toggle device toolbar" (Cmd-Shift-M on Mac). Confirm touch emulation is active (cursor becomes a circle). Or open the URL on an actual mobile device.

    14. One-finger touch and drag a canvas card. Confirm:
        - The card moves with your finger.
        - The viewport does NOT pan (no scroll happens).
        - TouchSensor activation requires 250ms hold + 5px tolerance (per BoardDragLayer.tsx sensor config); accept brief grace period.

    15. One-finger touch on an edge arrow and hold. Confirm:
        - The canvas pans.
        - No card is selected or dragged.

    16. Try simultaneously: touch a card with one finger, touch an arrow with another. Behavior should be deterministic (the gestures isolate via `stopPropagation`). Document any conflict.

    **Two-player sync verification**

    17. Open a second browser window or tab (separate profile to avoid token sharing) at `http://localhost:5173/?room=test35`. Join as "MobileTest2". Both players are in the same room.

    18. In window A (mobile viewport), drag a hand card to a specific spot on the canvas (e.g., x=200, y=100). In window B (desktop viewport, no max-h cap), confirm the card appears at the same canvas-space coordinates regardless of A's pan state.

    19. Pan window A right by ~100px. Confirm window B's view of the canvas is UNAFFECTED — scroll is client-only and must not broadcast.

    20. In window B (desktop), drag a card to the canvas. In window A (mobile, possibly panned), confirm the card appears at the broadcast position. If window A is panned, the new card may be partially or fully off-screen — that's correct behavior; use edge arrows to find it.

    **Final checks**

    21. Inspect the rendered `<EdgeArrow>` divs in DevTools. Confirm:
        - `aria-label="Pan canvas right"` (and equivalent for left/up/down).
        - `data-testid="edge-arrow-right"` (and equivalent for other directions).
        - Background: `rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.15)` with backdrop-filter blur(4px).
        - z-index: 1000.

    22. Run the full e2e suite once more to confirm nothing else regressed:
        `npm run test:e2e`
        (This may take ~120s. If it fails on a non-Phase-35 test, capture the failure and report.)
