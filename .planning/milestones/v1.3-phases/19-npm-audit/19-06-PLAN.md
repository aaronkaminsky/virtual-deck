Why z-20 specifically (not z-10, not z-50):
- shadcn Popover uses z-50 internally for the open popover panel. Header at z-20 stays above board content (which has no explicit z) but BELOW an open Popover panel — correct stacking for ControlsBar's open menu.
- z-20 is a single small step above the implicit z-0 of normal flow, providing a clean, intentional gap.

Why max-w-[200px] (not max-w-[180px], not max-w-[220px]):
- 5 × 42px CardBack = 210px of card content if laid edge-to-edge.
- With 4 overlap of -ml-3 (12px each): 42 + 4·30 = 162px for the visible strip.
- Badge (default shadcn — `inline-flex h-5 px-2 text-xs`) ≈ 20–24px wide for "5"–"99" content.
- gap-1 between strip and badge ≈ 4px.
- Outer container padding (rounded-lg p-1 from OpponentHand) ≈ 8px total horizontal.
- Sum: 162 + 24 + 4 + 8 = 198px. 200px provides exactly 2px of margin (sufficient — Tailwind rounds, browsers don't drift).
- 180px would still clip the badge ("5" displayed but trimmed border); 220px is unnecessary slack for a 375px viewport where two opponents already share `flex-1 overflow-x-auto`.

Why `flex-1 max-w-none` (not `w-full`) for the single-opponent case:
- The parent of the opponent map is `<div className="flex items-start gap-4 flex-1 overflow-x-auto">` (line 32). It uses `flex-1`, so its width is whatever remains after ControlsBar.
- A child with `flex-1` inside a `flex-row` parent grows to fill remaining space. This naturally caps at the parent width — no overflow risk.
- `w-full` would set the column to 100% of parent width, but the column is one item in a flex row with `gap-4` and may have siblings; `flex-1` is the idiomatic "fill remaining space" answer in a flex row.
- `max-w-none` cancels any conflicting `max-w-*` on the same element.
