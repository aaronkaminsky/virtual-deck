### Human Verification Required

#### 1. Post-drop dialog appears on pile drop

**Test:** In two browser tabs sharing a room, deal cards so both players have hands and piles have cards. Player A drags a card from hand and drops it onto the draw pile.
**Expected:** A modal dialog appears with three buttons labeled "Top", "Bottom", "Random".
**Why human:** Dialog rendering (Portal, Backdrop, Popup) requires a live browser environment.

#### 2. Bottom insertion — card is at position 0

**Test:** After the dialog appears, click "Bottom". Check the draw pile state in the second tab.
**Expected:** The card appears at the bottom of the pile (visually last in a face-down stack); the broadcast state shows it at index 0.
**Why human:** Requires two live browser tabs to verify server broadcast correctness and visual pile order.

#### 3. Escape dismiss defaults to Top

**Test:** Drop a card onto a pile. When the dialog appears, press Escape.
**Expected:** Dialog closes; card appears at the top of the pile for both players. No card is missing or in limbo.
**Why human:** Keyboard event triggers `onOpenChange(false, ...)` → `sendPendingMove('top')` — requires live interaction.

#### 4. Click-outside dismiss defaults to Top

**Test:** Drop a card onto a pile. When the dialog appears, click the backdrop (outside the dialog popup).
**Expected:** Same as Escape — card goes to Top, no limbo.
**Why human:** Pointer dismiss requires confirming `disablePointerDismissal` is false in the installed version of @base-ui/react Dialog (default behavior, but confirming in browser is safest).

#### 5. Hand drop suppresses dialog

**Test:** Drag a card from pile to your own hand (not another player's hand).
**Expected:** Card moves to hand immediately; no insert-position dialog appears.
**Why human:** The `toZone !== 'pile'` conditional in `handleDragEnd` suppresses the dialog — requires live drag to confirm the branch fires correctly.

#### 6. Random — position not revealed

**Test:** Drop a card onto a pile and click "Random". Observe both tabs.
**Expected:** Card appears in the pile at some position; neither player is shown which index was chosen.
**Why human:** Requires confirming the broadcast game state does not include the insertion index, and that the pile renders the card without revealing its position.
